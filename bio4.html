Simulation of Bile Action

Question

Q13: Explain the role of bile in digestion. Is it involved in chemical or mechanical digestion?

Section 3:
Simulation of Bile Action

The citrus juice stimulates bile movement in the small intestine by breaking down the vegetable oil, which then combines with the water in cup B. It's comparable to bile in the small intestine breaking down lipids into fatty acids. The purpose of the bile is to help in the digestion of lipids in the duodenum. Bile is a fluid produced and retained in the gallbladder by the liver that aids digestion and breaks down lipids into fatty acids. Emulsification is the action of bile on fat and is an example of mechanical digestion. Since humans eat so quickly, the amylase contained in saliva digests little carbs while food is in the mouth. Bile is a mechanical digestion that contributes to the surface area to volume ratio, making enzymes more likely to break down lipids entirely.
