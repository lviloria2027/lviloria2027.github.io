Section 1 Stages of Food Processing:
Transfer Stage

The liquid pouring from the stocking demonstrates osmosis and water absorption in the small intestines. The tray depicts the intestinal lumen. The feces are represented by the stuff left in the stocking. Pushing the partially hardened material from the tights demonstrates extraction. Once the stomach contents feel like a thick soup liquid, we cut a tiny hole in one corner and slowly press into the tights or stocking (small intestine). The small intestine is made up of several thin structures known as villi. These have a vast surface area for absorbing food molecules. We hold the tights over the tray and slowly squeeze the liquid out. The liquid in the tray indicates the nutrients that the body receives and utilizes. Food left behind the tights symbolizes waste items that cannot be digested.
